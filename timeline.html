Timeline
1st Generation (1967–1969)
  • Debuted as Chevrolet’s answer to the Ford Mustang.
  • Came with over 80 factory options, including the legendary SS and Z/28 trims.
  • The RS package included hidden headlights—very cool for the time.
  • The Z/28 was built to qualify for Trans-Am racing with a high-revving 302 V8.
  • Convertible versions were available right from the start.
2nd Generation (1970–1981)
  • Had a longer production run than any other Camaro generation.
  • The styling became more European-inspired, with a sleeker body.
  • 1977 marked the first year Camaro outsold the Mustang.
  • The Z/28 was dropped in 1975 but revived in 1977 due to fan demand.
  • Big bumpers appeared in 1974 to comply with federal crash standards.
3rd Generation (1982–1992)
  • First Camaro with fuel injection, a hatchback, and a 4-cylinder option.
  • Introduced the Camaro IROC-Z in 1985, named after the racing series.
  • Featured a fully redesigned chassis with MacPherson struts up front.
  • Was the first Camaro to offer a 5-speed manual transmission.
  • Convertible made a comeback in 1987 after a long hiatus.
4th Generation (1993–2002)
  • First Camaro to use plastic body panels (on the front fenders).
  • Shared the LT1 and later LS1 V8 engines with the Corvette.
  • Offered a “SS” model with SLP Engineering upgrades—making it a true sleeper.
  • Had the longest hood of any Camaro generation.
  • Final year (2002) marked Camaro’s 35th anniversary and the end—temporarily.
5th Generation (2010–2015)
  • Inspired heavily by the 1st-gen design—especially the front grille and stance.
  • Revived the Camaro name after an 8-year production hiatus.
  • ZL1 version packed a 580 hp supercharged V8.
  • Transformers’ Bumblebee made this Camaro a pop-culture icon.
  • Camaro won the World Car Design of the Year in 2010.
6th Generation (2016–2024)
  • Built on the lighter Alpha platform, shared with the Cadillac ATS.
  • More compact and nimble than the 5th-gen—great for performance driving.
  • Offered the mighty ZL1 1LE: track-focused and ready to battle supercars.
  • Last manual transmission Camaro offered (as of 2024).
  • 2024 marks the end of the Camaro (again), but Chevy hints at a future revival (Electric YUKKY).
